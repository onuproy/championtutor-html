Exam Strategies

Math tutors know the most important exam strategies that students should use to score excellent grades in tests and examinations. They will be able to guide students to follow the perfect techniques to secure a top rank with minimal mistakes. Such exam techniques include,

Skimming through the exam questions

It is important to skim through the questions the moment the invigilator says “start writing”. This gives students the idea as to which questions are easy and which are challenging. In the first glance, students should be able to gauge how much of time each question might take.

More time should be spent on challenging questions while easy questions should be completed as fast as possible. While taking a look at the questions, it's important to critically think about which topics are being tested.

Time management

Students need to try their best to buy as much time as possible for challenging questions. Time is the biggest enemy for students during their examinations. Math exams especially requires students to think critically within short period of time. Time management comes with lots of practice and patience.

Math tutors will train students by making them complete practice papers with a set time limit. They will practice repeatedly such that on the actual day of exam, there will be plenty of time to spare for checking. Most students make careless mistakes as they do not get the chance to rectify it. Should they have rechecked their answers before the time ended, those careless mistakes would have been spotted.

Calm mindset

Fear and panic will adversely affect the performance of students during examinations. A certain level of stress is healthy as it activates the “fight or flight response” which enables students to think critically and creatively. However, if stress exceeds optimal level, it will adversely affect their performance.

With a negative mindset, the easiest of questions would start to seem challenging. This causes students to lose their chance of even gaining the easy marks.

Presentation of answers

A neat presentation of answer will make it easier for examiners to award the marks easily without second thoughts. Neatness does not only refer to legible handwriting; it includes systematic presentation of answers as well.

The solution to mathematical problems typically has several steps to it. These intermediate steps are awarded “method marks” demarcated as “M1”, “M2” and so on. There are many more abbreviations that examiners use while marking a paper to breakdown and analyse the answer that has been derived by the student. Some other examples include,

  • A1 - accuracy mark
  • B1 - independent mark
  • E1 - mark for explaining
  • U1 - mark for correct units
  • G1 - mark for a correct feature on a graph
  • cao - correct answer only
  • ft - follow through
  • isw - ignore subsequent working
  • oe - or equivalent
  • rot - rounded or truncated
  • sc - special case
  • soi - seen or implied
Practice With Purpose

Blindly finishing one practice paper after another does not help improve grades. With every practice paper, students need to,

  • Analyse their mistakes
  • Check if their presentation is neat
  • Identify which topics they are weak in
  • Identify which topics are easy for them to handle
  • Analyse why they make certain type of careless mistakes
  • Identify knowledge gaps

These are not easy tasks and struggling alone can be daunting. It is hence best for students to seek help of school math teachers or private math tutors for guidance. When areas of weaknesses are easily identified, students can work on them in a targeted manner.

Every practice should have a purpose!
